📖 OVERVIEW

Space Debris Cleaner is a mobile VR game built with A-Frame (WebXR). You are the AI targeting system aboard an orbital defence station. Low-Earth orbit is filling with dangerous space junk — asteroids and broken satellites — that threaten the station.

Using only your gaze (no hands required), lock on to debris and vaporise it before it reaches you. But be careful: active, operational satellites share the same orbit — destroying one is a costly mistake!

Survive two increasingly difficult sectors to save the station.

Option A — GitHub Pages (recommended)

🎮 CONTROLS

This game is designed for hands-free gaze control — perfect for Google Cardboard and other mobile VR headsets.

ActionHow
Look around Physically move your head (gyroscope / device orientation on mobile)
or click-drag the mouse on desktop
Target debris Centre the orange reticle on an object
Zap debris Hold your gaze on the object for 1.5 seconds (normal) or 1.2 s (hard) — the reticle glows orange as it charges
Navigate menus Tap buttons on screen / touch-tap on mobile
Enter VR mode Tap the 👓 goggles button (bottom-right of scene)
🎯 Tip: The reticle ring turns orange and grows larger as you lock on. Release your gaze before it completes to cancel — useful for accidentally targeting a friendly satellite.

Lives

You start each level with 3 lives ❤️❤️❤️. If any debris (asteroid or broken satellite) reaches your position without being zapped, you lose one life. Active satellites drifting past you do not cost a life. Losing all 3 lives ends the game immediately.

Game Flow

  1. Flash Screen — Title card with creator name. Tap to proceed.
  2. Setup Menu — Toggle music, pick difficulty, read instructions.
  3. Level 1 (60 s) — Moderate speed, 73 % debris, 7 objects max.
  4. Level Complete — See your score, advance to Level 2.
  5. Level 2 (90 s) — Faster objects, more active satellites, up to 11 at once.
  6. Win / Game Over — Final score shown; play again anytime.

🥽 STEREOSCOPIC VR SETUP

  1. Open the game URL in Chrome on your Android or iOS phone.
  2. Complete the Flash Screen and Setup Menu (tap buttons on screen).
  3. Once the game is running, tap the VR goggles icon (bottom-right).
  4. Insert your phone into a Google Cardboard headset.
  5. The view splits into left/right stereoscopic halves automatically.
  6. Move your head to look around — the gyroscope controls the camera.
  7. Centre the reticle on debris and hold your gaze to zap!
📱 iOS users: You may need to grant motion sensor permission. A prompt will appear on first load — tap Allow.
🖥 Desktop testing: Click and drag to rotate the view. All gaze mechanics work the same way — just use your mouse instead of your head.
